How do proteins fold into their 3D structure and what determines their final shape?

2:30 PM
B

Protein folding is determined by several factors:

1. Primary structure: The sequence of amino acids forms the backbone.

2. Hydrogen bonding: Creates secondary structures like alpha helices and beta sheets.

3. Hydrophobic interactions: Non-polar amino acids cluster in the center, away from water.

4. Ionic interactions: Charged amino acids form salt bridges.

5. Disulfide bridges: Covalent bonds between cysteine residues stabilize the structure.

The final 3D structure represents the most energetically favorable conformation.

Source: Biology 302 Textbook, Chapter 4: Protein Structure
